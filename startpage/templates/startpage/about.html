About NDLUG

Purpose

Our goal is to spread knowledge about GNU/Linux, the open source community, and software as a whole. The club seeks to be a relaxed and friendly space where people of all disciplines can gain and share information about software development, tech news, and interesting projects.

Meetings

We hold meetings every week on Thursdays from 6pm to 7pm in Debartolo Hall, room 224. We alternate between having a presentation week and a fun week. During the presentation week, one of the club members gives a presentation about a given topic for that week. Example topics have been on text editors, Linux distributions, side projects, the Tor project, etc. Topics are chosen by the club during the prior week's meeting. Any club member can give a presentation, and we encourage any and all members to participate. There is no requirement to come to weekly meetings, and you can come and go during the meetings as you please.

Life of the Lug

Lug is different from other clubs in that a lot of the activity exists online. We use the Notre Dame Slack channel for almost all our communication. Anyone with a nd.edu email address can join. Many of the club decisions, activities planning, and general camaraderie happens there. Slack has an IRC gateway which many of us use (in sticking with our Linux roots!) with our IRC clients to connect to the channel. We encourage all club members to join.
